# Hellpiercers Developer Clarifications

Supplement to the official errata at [hellpiercers.com/#errata](https://hellpiercers.com/#errata).

Sourced from Sandy Pug Games (Nemesis) responses on the [itch.io community forum](https://sandypuggames.itch.io/hellpiercers/community), crawled July 2026. Entries here are **not duplicated** in the official errata as of that date.

For book text and structured errata, prefer the PDF rulebook and the official errata page. Use this document when implementing edge cases the errata does not cover.

---

## Campaign & Table Setup

### Recommended player count

**Source:** [Number of Players recommended?](https://itch.io/t/4913545/number-of-players-recommended) — May 25, 2025

Almost every playtest used one GM and 3–4 players. Variable Action Economy is designed to ease 2-player teams, but encounters should be built with that balance in mind.

## Armor

### Asmodel — Physical Edification damage

**Source:** [Damage of Asmodel's Armor Action?](https://itch.io/t/5488605/damage-of-asmodels-armor-action) — Nov 2025

Physical Edification converts **remaining unspent Speed into damage** for its Burst:1 attack, then resets Speed to default. This is intentional.

If the burst feels too weak at the table, the developer suggests adding **Push:1** as a house tweak. Design intent: the armor pinballs around and creates space when out of formation.

## Swarms

### Movement speed (intentional nerf)

**Source:** [Quick questions that came up while playing](https://itch.io/t/5599393/quick-questions-that-came-up-while-playing) — Nov 30, 2025

Swarms were nerfed from playtest values because they were too fast and hard to create space around. Intended play pattern:

- Enemies collect around the party, trigger a big swarm, surround and block egress, pile on damage.
- Burst movement: one limb throws out to cut a line, a direct stretch, then fill in space on arrival.

Breaker counters them strongly at range; lack of movement buffs means they may not feel as explosive as intended. Consider pairing swarms with minibosses that enhance their kit.

**Proposed errata buff** (developer said they would add; not yet on the official errata page):

| Swarm size (units) | Bonus Speed |
|--------------------|-------------|
| 1–7                | +3          |
| 8–15               | +2          |
| (larger)           | +1          |

## Blazing

### Not primarily a swarm multiplier

**Source:** [Quick questions that came up while playing](https://itch.io/t/5599393/quick-questions-that-came-up-while-playing) — Nov 30, 2025

Blazing is **not** meant to be a super swarm-focused mechanic. It turns very large swarms into danger zones for the GM and discourages keeping enemies unswarmed in a "death ball" to hound players. Effective swarming is a natural counter to Blazing.

## Attractors & Voids

### Simultaneous pulls (wedged between two attractors)

**Source:** [Quick questions that came up while playing](https://itch.io/t/5599393/quick-questions-that-came-up-while-playing) — Nov 30, 2025

When a unit is pulled by multiple Void Attractors at once, **balancing the "gravity" of the two** is the more interesting read, but both interpretations (resolve in favor of one side vs. cancel out) are valid. A third attractor creates three-body-problem physics — try it and see.

### Sharur limit break vs. other players' attractor tiles

**Source:** [Quick questions that came up while playing](https://itch.io/t/5599393/quick-questions-that-came-up-while-playing) — Nov 30, 2025

RAW, the limit break should remove **all** attractor tiles, including another Sharur user's. The developer considers **not removing another player's tiles** an easy and reasonable table concession.

Suggested future tweak: let the limit-break user **absorb attractors by moving over them** for a short window, with a buff to damage or pull range per devoured attractor so two Sharur users can feed each other.

## Provoke

### Moving away from a multi-tile swarm

**Source:** [Quick questions that came up while playing](https://itch.io/t/5599393/quick-questions-that-came-up-while-playing) — Nov 30, 2025

If a unit is adjacent to multiple tiles of a swarm (e.g. surrounded on three sides) and moves away, the developer prefers **one Provoke roll per adjacent swarm tile** (e.g. 3d6 when surrounded on three sides) — the meaner option.

Note: official errata covers forced movement, armor-triggered movement immunity, and multi-Provoke ordering, but not this multi-adjacency swarm case.

## Classes & AGNOSIA

### Malakbel baseline speed

**Source:** [Quick questions that came up while playing](https://itch.io/t/5599393/quick-questions-that-came-up-while-playing) — Nov 30, 2025

Malakbel is intended as the **Fast Armor**, but getting far ahead of the squad and waiting is annoying — especially with Sprint (Aux) and when paired with slower builds (e.g. Kushiel). Large speed gaps are very significant in play.

### Theletus faction AGNOSIA

**Source:** [Quick questions that came up while playing](https://itch.io/t/5599393/quick-questions-that-came-up-while-playing) — Nov 30, 2025

Most Theletus units **do not have AGNOSIA effects**. The faction author planned a faction-wide special AGNOSIA mechanic but never finished it; several minibosses lack the form change. Ice-In-The-Veins having no AGNOSIA effect is an oversight, not intentional design. A faction-wide "drowsy" form is a reasonable GM/table fix until official content lands.

### AUTOPHYES Harvester AGNOSIA spawns

**Source:** [Quick questions that came up while playing](https://itch.io/t/5599393/quick-questions-that-came-up-while-playing) — Nov 30, 2025

When Harvester's AGNOSIA splits the unit:

| Trigger timing        | New entities act?                          |
|-----------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| Before Harvester's turn | Both new entities get turns that round |
| After Harvester's turn  | Wait until next round                  |
| During Harvester's turn | Wait until next round                  |

**Spawner-created units** can move and act on the same turn they spawn.

## Weapons

### She Speaks The Language of Kings — halved damage

**Source:** [Quick questions that came up while playing](https://itch.io/t/5599393/quick-questions-that-came-up-while-playing) — Nov 30, 2025

The active ability's "half damage" is **intentionally left open**:

- **Roll then halve** the result (developer's default), or
- **Half of maximum damage** (higher power level)

Choose based on table taste.

## STRATCOM & Hazards

### Map Hazards (beyond Devolve)

**Source:** [Quick questions that came up while playing](https://itch.io/t/5599393/quick-questions-that-came-up-while-playing) — Nov 30, 2025

**Devolve** is the only explicitly named Map Hazard in the core book. The original plan was one unique hazard per faction; authors diverged (e.g. the large snake in MIXIS). The STRATCOM "build an unlocked map hazard" action was meant to become a **PbTA-style GM Move** — an arbitrary narrative tool — but the expanded write-up was never finished.

Until expansion content exists, treat Map Hazards narratively at the table (river overflows, toxic gas cloud, etc.).

### Faction unit availability

**Source:** [Quick questions that came up while playing](https://itch.io/t/5599393/quick-questions-that-came-up-while-playing) — Nov 30, 2025; [Not clear which units I can use as GM before unlocking](https://itch.io/t/5199382/not-clear-which-units-i-can-use-as-gm-before-unlocking) — Aug 10, 2025 (community report; no official reply)

The book does not clearly mark which enemy units require faction unlocks vs. are available from the start. GMs must cross-reference faction upgrade trees (e.g. Singing Scythe, Whaler). A per-unit "requirement" field would help; none exists in the current text.
